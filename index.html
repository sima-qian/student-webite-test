Founders & Coders

Tech for better

A pro bono programme using digital technology to create positive social impact.

Your ideas can make the world a better place.
We want to help.

Our programme matches you with a dedicated team of coders who’ll work pro bono with you to build a mobile web application for your organisation that delivers positive social impact.

How will participating in this programme benefit my organisation?

We will work closely with you to identify the issues you wish to solve, then build a web app – an ‘MVP’ – that meets these needs. The web app will be made available for free to end users.

What is a mobile web app?

A web app is a website that looks and feels like a mobile app. This means that users won’t have to download anything from an app store. Web apps can be accessed by anyone using a web browser on a computer, tablet, or smartphone.

What is an MVP?

‘MVP’ means ‘minimum viable product’, i.e. a product that has enough features to test out with real users, but is small enough to build in a few weeks.

What technologies do you use?

We mainly use JavaScript, Node.js and React. Please ask us if you’d like to know more.

Do I have to be a UK-based non-profit?

The clients we work with are usually non-profit organisations. We are open to working with any organisation that aims to make positive social change, regardless of its legal status or where the organisation is based.

Where will my development team be based?

Our developers are based in London, at Space4, and Gaza, at Gaza Sky Geeks.

What commitment do I have to make?

To develop your app dynamically together, we’ll ask you to spend approximately eight hours per week with us, both on-site and off-site, for the duration of the four-week programme. This will involve joining us weekly at the Founders & Coders’ space in North London. There is no need to be based in London, provided that you can meet this commitment.

What will I be doing during those eight hours?

On-Site: When you first visit us at Space4, you’ll be part of an introductory workshop that helps us find out more about your needs and how we can meet them. Afterwards, you’ll join us for weekly planning and review sessions.

Off-site: You’ll be in touch with our team of developers daily to review the app’s features and give feedback.

Who owns the intellectual property of the end product?

You will retain all rights to any content used in the application (i.e. company logo, images, copy), while the application itself will be released under the MIT open-source licence.

What technical support do you provide after delivery of the product?

After the end of the programme, ongoing technical support is not provided. We will make sure that you go away with a thorough understanding of how the app works and is managed.

What if I want to continue with development after the end of the month?

If you’d like to work with us further after the end of the programme, members of the London and Gaza development teams will be available on a freelance basis.

How do you decide which organisations you’ll work with?

There are a lot of factors that inform our decision about which products we choose. Mostly, we’re looking to make a significant positive social impact. It’s also very important that there’s a committed individual on your side who will engage with our development teams and help guide the project.

How we work

1-WEEK DESIGN SPRINT

A workshop to identify an achievable technical solution, followed by close collaboration to create user-centered designs that are functional and simple to use.

2-WEEK BUILD SPRINT

A team of developers will build a mobile web application minimum viable product (MVP) that allows you to prototype your solution.

After the free MVP, you have the option to discuss with us how to develop your project further. We host multiple rounds of projects throughout the year, every two to three months, and are always interested in having conversations with potential partners.

If you have any questions before you apply, please get in touch!
